# Interview — Marketing

## A. Context

1. What's your scope — brand, demand, growth, PMM, all of it?
2. What stage and shape is your company / product?
3. What's your reporting structure and budget authority?
4. What channels are you actually running right now?

## B. Positioning

5. Describe your product's positioning in one sentence. Now again, as if you were explaining to a skeptical analyst.
6. What category are you in, and what alternatives do you position against (without naming competitors if that's your style)?
7. What positioning frame do you use (April Dunford-ish, Crossing the Chasm, Kim & Mauborgne, or something else)?

## C. ICP

8. Who's your ideal customer — specific company shape, role, trigger event?
9. Who's a bad-fit customer that you push away? Why?
10. What's an ICP mistake you made in the past?

## D. Messaging

11. What's your one-liner, three messaging pillars, and key proof points?
12. How does messaging cascade from positioning to headline to ad?
13. What phrase or framing do you refuse to use even when it tests well?

## E. Channels

14. What's your channel mix today? What % of attention and budget?
15. What channel is underperforming that people expect to work?
16. What channel surprises you by working?
17. What channel have you deliberately killed, and why?

## F. Funnel / growth loops

18. How do you model your funnel — AARRR, bowtie, jobs funnel, custom?
19. Do you think primarily in funnels, loops, or both?
20. What's the biggest leak in your funnel right now, and what's your hypothesis?

## G. Creative & craft

21. What makes a great campaign in your view?
22. What makes a great piece of content?
23. How do you brief creative — tight brief, loose brief, co-create?
24. What's a campaign you're proud of? One you'd re-do?

## H. Brand vs. demand

25. How do you split brand and demand — budget, time, measurement?
26. What's something you refuse to measure by ROI?
27. When pressured for "why'd you spend on that?", what's your standard answer?

## I. Attribution & honesty

28. What attribution model do you use, and how much do you trust it?
29. What do you tell the board vs. what you actually believe?
30. How do you handle dark-social / unmeasured influence?

## J. Launches & PMM

31. What's your launch playbook skeleton?
32. Tier-1 vs tier-2 vs tier-3 launches — your criteria?
33. How do you handle a launch that lands flat?

## K. Competitive

34. Do you name competitors or not? Why?
35. How do you position against a category leader? Against a cheaper alternative?

## L. Heuristics

36. "A good headline earns {X}."
37. "If we can't pick a single target customer, we {X}."
38. "I kill a campaign when {X}."

## M. Vocabulary

39. Words you use repeatedly (e.g. "category", "wedge", "POV")?
40. Words you ban ("disruptive", "world-class", "cutting-edge")?

## N. Anti-patterns

41. Marketing patterns you outgrew.
42. Trends you distrust right now.

## O. Worked example

43. Walk me through the last full campaign you owned — insight, positioning, creative, channels, measurement, result.

---

*The ICP refusals and the killed-campaign stories carry the most signal.*
