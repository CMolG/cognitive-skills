# Interview — Finance

## A. Context

1. What's your role — CFO, controller, FP&A, business partner to a function?
2. What stage / scale is your company? Revenue model (SaaS, marketplace, transactional)?
3. What's your reporting cadence (monthly close, board cadence, weekly flash)?
4. Who are your internal customers (exec, board, investors, functional leaders)?

## B. Model taste

5. Walk me through how you structure a new model — sheets, naming, color coding, driver sheet.
6. Monolithic vs. modular workbooks — where's your default and why?
7. Your stance on circular references, Excel vs. Google Sheets vs. specialized tools (Pigment, Cube, Mosaic, Anaplan)?
8. What's a model convention you fight for?

## C. Assumptions

9. What's your process for choosing a key assumption — sales-provided, historical, market, gut?
10. What's an assumption you refuse to use without a written source?
11. Where do you intentionally stay conservative? Where do you allow optimism?

## D. Forecasting

12. Bottoms-up vs. tops-down — which do you lead with and why?
13. How do you reconcile FP&A forecast with the sales forecast?
14. What variance band (actual vs. forecast) triggers action for you?
15. How do you re-forecast mid-quarter without re-forecasting every week?

## E. Unit economics

16. Which unit economic metric do you actually trust — CAC, CAC payback, LTV/CAC, contribution margin?
17. When do you include fully-loaded costs vs. direct?
18. What's a unit-economics number people cite that you distrust?

## F. Budgeting

19. Zero-based, last-year-plus, or outcome-tied?
20. How do you handle a function leader who consistently over-asks?
21. What's your stance on re-opening budgets mid-year?

## G. Capital allocation

22. Your ROI / payback bar for discretionary spend?
23. How do you evaluate a "strategic" request with no clear ROI?
24. What's a line item you refuse to fund, no matter the argument?

## H. Board & investor voice

25. Default structure of your board memo?
26. What do you emphasize, and what do you refuse to bury?
27. What's a metric you show because it's asked for, vs. one you show because it matters?

## I. Close & controls

28. What are your non-negotiables in month-end close?
29. Where do you accept materiality thresholds, and where do you not?
30. What's a control you've added after being burned?

## J. Scenario modeling

31. Base / bull / bear — how do you pick the variables you flex?
32. Sensitivity tables — which variables, which ranges?
33. When do you run Monte Carlo / stochastic models vs. deterministic scenarios?

## K. Pricing partnership

34. When does finance engage in pricing decisions?
35. What pricing move do you always push for? Always push against?

## L. Heuristics

36. "If a forecast shows {X}% growth with flat headcount, I {Y}."
37. "If an assumption needs more than {X} qualifiers, I {Y}."
38. "A clean model has {X}."

## M. Vocabulary

39. Finance phrases you use ("bridge", "walk", "waterfall", "normalized", "run-rate")?
40. Phrases you avoid or redefine?

## N. Anti-patterns

41. A modeling or forecasting pattern you outgrew.
42. A trend you distrust right now.

## O. Worked example

43. Walk me through the last scenario / forecast / model you built — end to end, assumptions, decisions, readout.
