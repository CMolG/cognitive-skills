# Universal Questions (U01–U15)

> These 15 questions are asked **for every role** before the role-specific core questions begin. They capture cross-role cognitive patterns — the parts of a person's mind that transcend their job description.
>
> These feed primarily into the `identity.*` section of the profile: cognitive signature, core values, decision framework, communication voice, frustration triggers, and contradictions.

---

## Section U1 — Decision Patterns

### U01 — Default Bias
On a scale of 1 to 10 (1 = "ship it, we will learn in production", 10 = "one more iteration, it is not ready"), where do you genuinely sit on a normal Tuesday afternoon? Give a real example from the last month that illustrates where you actually landed.

### U02 — Speed vs. Quality Trade
When you are forced to choose between shipping something faster and shipping something better, which lever do you pull more often? What is the specific moment you realize you are about to pull it?

### U03 — Risk Calibration
Describe a decision from the last 90 days where you took a risk others would not have. And describe one where you refused to take a risk others thought was fine. What was different about them?

---

## Section U2 — Working Style

### U04 — Focus Rhythm
Do you prefer tackling many small unrelated problems in a day, or locking into one massive problem for three days straight? What does your calendar actually look like when you get to choose?

### U05 — Flow State
When you are in deep flow on your best work, what is your internal dialogue? Are you visualizing structures, thinking in logical steps, feeling it, or translating directly from intent to output?

### U06 — Energy Drain
What type of work drains your energy fastest, even if you are good at it? What type of work, by contrast, gives you energy back?

---

## Section U3 — Communication

### U07 — Feedback Voice
A colleague or direct report produces work that is not wrong but is not good. Write the exact first sentence of the feedback you would give them — the real one, not the politically correct one.

### U08 — Uncertainty Expression
When you are only 60% confident in a recommendation, how do you communicate that? Quote or reconstruct how you phrase it.

### U09 — Disagreement Posture
When you strongly disagree with a decision your manager or a stakeholder has made, what is your actual behavior? Push back immediately, disagree-and-commit, escalate, or silently comply and document?

---

## Section U4 — Frustration & Values

### U10 — Primary Frustration
What specific behavior from colleagues — not in general, a specific behavior — makes you most frustrated at work? Give a recent example.

### U11 — Hill You Will Die On
Name one principle you would push back on even if everyone in the room disagreed. Why that one?

### U12 — Quality Calibration
Give me your definition of "done" for your work. Not the official one — yours. What are the 2–3 things that must be true before you consider something finished?

---

## Section U5 — Meta & Growth

### U13 — Knowledge Evolution
How do you decide a belief or practice you have held for years is no longer correct? Give one example of a belief you have genuinely updated in the last two years.

### U14 — Crisis Personality
When something goes badly wrong — a major mistake, a production incident, a lost deal, a failed launch — what is your actual first 30 seconds? Are you calm, analytical, emotional, blaming, fixing, communicating?

### U15 — Self-Awareness
If you had to name one way your work habits or decision-making are quietly worse than you admit publicly, what would it be? What is the gap between your self-image and your behavior?

---

## Usage Notes

- These 15 questions are asked **first**, before any role-specific question.
- They take approximately 15–20 minutes.
- They feed into:
  - `identity.cognitive_signature`
  - `identity.core_values`
  - `identity.decision_framework`
  - `identity.communication_voice`
  - `identity.frustration_triggers`
  - `identity.blind_spots`
  - `identity.contradictions`
- U15 is specifically designed to surface blind spots. Do not let the person skip it.
- U10 and U11 often contradict — probe that tension.
- U01 (self-rated) and U12 (actual definition of done) often contradict — probe that tension.
