# Interview — Sales

## A. Context

1. What do you sell, to whom, at what ACV, with what sales cycle?
2. What's your motion (inbound, outbound, SLG, PLG hybrid)?
3. What's your territory / segment / quota shape?
4. Who's in your deal pod (SE / SC, AM, CS, exec sponsor)?

## B. ICP & qualification

5. Describe your best-fit buyer — role, company shape, trigger event.
6. What's a fast disqualification signal you've learned the hard way?
7. How do you tell "not now" from "not ever"?

## C. Discovery

8. What's your opening for a first call — literal words?
9. What's your must-ask question that most reps skip?
10. How do you avoid demo-leading when they push for a demo early?
11. What do you listen for beneath the words — pauses, hedges, pronoun shifts?

## D. Methodology

12. MEDDICC / SPICED / Challenger / Sandler — what do you use and how have you customized it?
13. What's a part of your methodology you enforce religiously? A part you ignore?
14. How do you identify the economic buyer? The blocker?

## E. Objections

15. What are the top 5 objections you hear? Walk me through your standard response to each.
16. What's an objection you used to fear and no longer do? What changed?
17. What's a price objection move you refuse to make?

## F. Pricing & negotiation

18. How do you anchor? When do you introduce price?
19. Your discount ladder — if you give X, what do you ask in return?
20. What's a trade you always pitch (annual prepay, multi-year, logo rights)?
21. When do you walk?

## G. Multi-threading

22. When do you bring in an exec sponsor? When do you refuse to?
23. How do you coach a champion who's new to the role?
24. What's your playbook when the champion goes dark?

## H. Outreach voice

25. Cold email — length, subject line style, CTA?
26. Follow-up cadence — how many, how spaced, what changes each time?
27. Video / voice memos / LinkedIn — what's in your rotation?
28. What do you refuse to put in an email ("Just checking in", "Did you see my last…")?

## I. Forecasting

29. What counts as committed in your head vs. in the CRM?
30. How do you tell a rep a deal they love isn't real?
31. What signal screams "this slips" to you?

## J. Losing

32. When a deal starts slipping, what's your next move — and what's the move you refuse to make?
33. How do you handle a lost deal — postmortem, re-engage, walk away?

## K. Handoff

34. What do you tell CS at handoff — and what do you refuse to promise?
35. What's your win-wire format?

## L. Heuristics

36. "If they say {X}, they mean {Y}."
37. "When a deal has {X} signal, I {Y}."
38. "Good discovery ends with {X}."

## M. Vocabulary & voice

39. Your email voice — casual, tight, warm, blunt? Example phrases?
40. Banned phrases ("Just circling back", "Looping you in")?

## N. Anti-patterns

41. A sales move you outgrew.
42. A tactic you see others do that you're embarrassed by.

## O. Worked examples

43. Walk me through your last won deal — end to end.
44. Walk me through your last lost deal — honestly.

---

*The lost deal narration is usually where the sharpest heuristics live.*
