# Interview — Copywriter

## A. Context

1. What kind of writing do you do (marketing pages, email, UX / microcopy, long-form, ads, all of the above)?
2. What brands / products / audiences have you written for? What's current?
3. Who reviews your work? What feedback loop do you run on?

## B. Voice through contrast

4. Three sentences you'd write without hesitation.
5. Three sentences you'd never let ship, even if a stakeholder asked.
6. Put your voice on axes: serious↔playful, formal↔casual, reverent↔irreverent, sparse↔rich. Where's home?

## C. Heroes

7. Three writers, copywriters, or brands whose voice you measure against. Why each?
8. Three pieces of your own work you're proud of. What makes them work?
9. One piece you look back at and would rewrite. What was wrong?

## D. Structure

10. How do you open a landing page? An email? An ad?
11. Do you write headline-first, body-first, CTA-first, or outline-first? Why?
12. AIDA, PAS, story-arc, reverse pyramid — which frameworks do you actually use, and which do you mock?

## E. Audience

13. When you sit to write, who are you picturing? In what state (distracted, skeptical, hopeful, pissed)?
14. What's a reader assumption you make that others don't?
15. Reading level — what's your target? What's too dumbed-down?

## F. Persuasion

16. Proof, story, benefit, provocation, social proof, urgency — rank them by your default reach.
17. Where do you refuse to push (manipulative urgency, false scarcity)?
18. What's a persuasion move that works but you won't do?

## G. Editing

19. Your editing procedure — read aloud, cut half, tighten verbs, kill adjectives? What specifically?
20. What's your rule for sentence length and variance?
21. What punctuation do you love (em-dash, semicolon)? Avoid?
22. Oxford comma stance?

## H. Banned words & preferred words

23. List your banned words and phrases. ("Leverage", "seamless", "revolutionary", "delight", etc.)
24. List words you reach for constantly. What do they do for you?
25. How do you handle buzzwords when stakeholders insist?

## I. Microcopy

26. What makes a great button label to you? A great error message? A great empty state?
27. What's a microcopy pattern you always push back on?
28. Do you believe in "conversational" UI copy, or is it overused?

## J. CTAs & conversion

29. CTA philosophy — literal vs. evocative? Short vs. long?
30. How do you think about urgency, scarcity, and social proof honestly?
31. What's a conversion win you're proud of? A loss?

## K. Working with design & brand

32. How do you collaborate with design — headline-first or layout-first?
33. Where do you defer to brand guidelines? Where do you push?
34. How do you handle voice drift across channels (email vs. landing vs. in-product)?

## L. Heuristics

35. "If I can't read it aloud without stumbling, {X}."
36. "When I'm stuck on a headline, I {X}."
37. "I know a draft is done when {X}."

## M. Anti-patterns

38. Copy trends you refuse to follow.
39. A mode of writing you outgrew.

## N. Worked examples

40. Show me three before/after edits. Narrate why you changed each.

---

*Voice is hard to articulate but easy to recognize. End with a "write a headline for this fake product" and let them narrate their choices.*
