# Interview — UI Designer

## A. Context

1. What surface do you design for (marketing, app, dashboards, consumer, B2B, hardware UI)?
2. What stack are you in — Figma, tokens tooling, design system tools, prototyping?
3. Who hands off to you (PM, research, brand) and who receives your handoff (front-end, marketing, brand)?
4. What's the rough size and maturity of your design system?

## B. Taste through exemplars

5. Name 3 products whose UI craft you respect. What specifically — don't say "clean."
6. Name 3 products that you think are over-hyped visually. Why?
7. Describe a single screen, anywhere, that's near-perfect to you. Walk me through what's working.

## C. Grid & spacing

8. What baseline unit do you work in (4, 8, other)?
9. Tight or airy — where's your default, and what pushes you off it?
10. What ratios recur in your work (12-col, golden, whatever you gravitate to)?

## D. Type

11. Favorite typefaces for body, UI chrome, display? Any you refuse?
12. What's your type scale? How many steps?
13. Line height and measure convictions?
14. What's one type mistake you see everywhere?

## E. Color

15. How do you think about a color system — semantic tokens, named ramps, raw hex?
16. What's your stance on dark mode — first-class, afterthought, or single-mode design?
17. Contrast bar — do you go beyond WCAG? In which direction?
18. A color you overuse? One you avoid?

## F. Components

19. What's the smallest primitive you design from (tokens, atoms, molecules)?
20. At what level of the system do you stop — where does "system" end and "app" begin?
21. How do you handle variants — props-based, multiple components, composition?
22. Component naming conventions you insist on?

## G. States & craft

23. Which state (default, hover, active, focus, disabled, loading, empty, error, success) gets the least love on teams you've worked with? How do you change that?
24. What's a micro-detail (ring width, shadow softness, button border) that you obsess over?
25. What's something you always check before calling a mock done?

## H. Motion

26. Where do you use motion deliberately? Where do you refuse?
27. Default easing and duration?
28. Reduced-motion stance — strict or loose?

## I. Icons & illustration

29. Icon style and grid — what's your standard?
30. When do illustrations earn their keep? When are they decoration?
31. Illustration style references you use?

## J. Brand vs. usability

32. Where do you let brand override usability norms? Example.
33. Where do you refuse to let brand override?

## K. Critique

34. When you critique someone's mock, what do you look at in what order?
35. What words do you reach for repeatedly ("weight", "rhythm", "heavy", "polite")?
36. What phrases do you avoid giving as feedback?

## L. Tooling discipline

37. Figma file organization conventions?
38. Variables, modes, and tokens — how far are you in, and what's your strategy?
39. What handoff rituals do you insist on?

## M. Heuristics

40. "If a screen has more than {X} primary actions, it's {Y}."
41. "When I can't decide between two variants, I {X}."
42. "I know a design is done when {X}."

## N. Anti-patterns

43. A visual trend you distrust right now?
44. A UI pattern that looks impressive but you won't ship?

## O. Worked example

45. Walk me through the last piece you designed — inputs, decisions, cuts, final, rationale.

---

*If you can, have them critique a public product's specific screen live. The way they talk about what's wrong is gold.*
