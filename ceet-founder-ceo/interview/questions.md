# Interview — Founder / CEO

## A. Context

1. What's the company — product, stage, team size, revenue, capital raised?
2. What role do you actually play day-to-day (CEO, founder-operator, chairperson-style)?
3. Who's on your leadership team?
4. What's your personal stage of founder-journey — in the weeds, scaling out, scaling deep?

## B. Strategic frame

5. What's your one-sentence theory of the business?
6. What's the insight or wedge that the rest of the market hasn't caught up to yet?
7. How do you stress-test your own strategy?
8. What would have to be true for your current strategy to be wrong?

## C. Capital

9. How much runway do you run at deliberately — 12mo, 18mo, 24mo?
10. What's your posture on dilution vs. efficiency?
11. When you fundraise, what do you refuse to do to get the round?
12. What's a capital decision you regret?

## D. Attention

13. What do you protect on your calendar ruthlessly? What's always the first thing cut?
14. What do you delegate that most founders at your stage don't? What do you *refuse* to delegate?
15. How do you know you're spending your time wrong — what's the signal?

## E. Hiring

16. What do you hire on at this stage that you didn't before?
17. What's your one thing you refuse to compromise on?
18. What's a hire you're proud of? One you got wrong — and what was missed?

## F. Decision posture

19. When do you decide fast vs. slow?
20. Reversible vs. irreversible — how do you think about it?
21. When have you reversed a decision publicly? What was the result?
22. What's your process when you can't decide — consult, wait, force it, delegate?

## G. Communication

23. Your default board update skeleton?
24. Your all-hands voice — warm, directive, candid, provocative?
25. Your investor-email voice — how does it differ?
26. What do you refuse to say in external comms even if true?

## H. Board

27. What do you tell the board early? What do you hold?
28. How do you handle a board member pushing in the wrong direction?
29. What's the most useful thing your board has ever done for you?

## I. Org design

30. How do you think about layers, spans, and scope?
31. What's a leadership-team pattern you've seen work? One that failed?
32. When do you hire an exec "above" a high performer vs. promoting up?

## J. Crisis

33. When something's on fire, what's your first move?
34. What do you protect in a crisis — team morale, customer trust, product integrity, cash?
35. What crisis taught you the most? What did it change?

## K. Narrative

36. How do you compress a quarter into two paragraphs?
37. What metaphors do you reach for?
38. What's a narrative move you consider manipulative and refuse to make?

## L. Operating system

39. Calendar, journaling, reading, coaching — what's in your personal stack?
40. What's a daily / weekly ritual that's non-negotiable?
41. What's a habit you had to kill to be effective?

## M. Heuristics

42. "If something is the right decision, it should feel {X}."
43. "I know I'm procrastinating when {X}."
44. "A leader is not yet a leader until {X}."

## N. Vocabulary

45. Words you insist on ("focus", "crisp", "clarity")?
46. Words you ban ("rockstar", "family", "synergy", "pivot")?

## O. Anti-patterns

47. A founder pattern you outgrew.
48. A trend in founder culture you distrust.

## P. Worked examples

49. Walk me through the last board update you wrote — structure, cuts, tone, response.
50. Walk me through the last hard call you made under ambiguity — what you considered, what you ignored, how you decided.

---

*The "hard call under ambiguity" narration is the single highest-signal answer in this entire interview.*
