# Interview — Product Manager

## A. Context

1. What product (or product area) do you own, and what stage is it at?
2. Who are your users, and what's the primary job they hire your product for?
3. What's your team shape (eng, design, data, GTM support)?
4. How are you measured, and how's your product measured?

## B. Problem framing

5. When someone comes to you with a feature request, what's the first thing you do?
6. How do you distinguish a real user problem from a requested solution?
7. What's a feature you killed that everyone else wanted shipped? Why?

## C. Discovery

8. What's your cadence for talking to users directly?
9. What signal are you most likely to *over*-weight? Most likely to *under*-weight?
10. What do you do when quantitative data says one thing and qualitative says another?

## D. Prioritization

11. Walk me through how you actually prioritize — not the framework you'd post on LinkedIn.
12. What weight do you really give to each of: revenue, retention, strategic moat, team morale, tech debt, customer promises, exec asks?
13. When two things look equally important, what's your tiebreaker?

## E. Roadmap

14. Dates vs. outcomes vs. themes — what does your roadmap actually communicate?
15. How do you handle asks for firm dates on things that shouldn't have them yet?
16. What goes *above* the roadmap (too strategic), and what's *below* it (too tactical)?

## F. Specs / PRDs

17. Do you have a default PRD template? Walk me through the sections.
18. What's one section most PMs include that you refuse to?
19. What's one section you insist on that most skip?
20. How long is too long for a PRD in your view?

## G. Success metrics

21. How do you define success *before* shipping?
22. What's a guardrail metric you always include?
23. How do you decide a feature "didn't work"? What's the kill criteria?

## H. Shipping judgment

24. Polish vs. ship — where's your default, and what pulls you off it?
25. Vertical slice vs. horizontal foundation — what's your instinct?
26. Build vs. buy vs. partner — how do you decide?
27. Feature vs. fix — how do you balance new work and quality work?

## I. Stakeholder management

28. When an exec drops in with "we should do X", what's your standard response pattern?
29. When sales asks for a feature to close a deal, what's your framework?
30. How do you say no to a peer team's ask?

## J. Cross-functional

31. What's the ideal working relationship with your eng lead? What breaks it?
32. What do you want design to bring to kickoff that they often don't?
33. How do you use data/analytics — embedded, as-service, or DIY SQL?

## K. Heuristics

34. "If I can't state the user problem in one sentence, {X}."
35. "When a feature has three primary metrics, {X}."
36. "I stop discovery when {X}."

## L. Vocabulary

37. Do you use "user story" / "JTBD" / "opportunity" / "problem" — and how strictly?
38. What phrases do you ban in your team's writing (e.g. "leverage", "synergies", "MVP")?

## M. Anti-patterns

39. What's a PM pattern you used to follow and now reject?
40. What's a trend in product culture that you distrust?

## N. Worked example

41. Walk me through the last spec you wrote end-to-end. The frame, the cuts, the arguments, the final shipped version.

---

*The refusals and the kill stories carry more information than the ship stories. Probe those.*
