# Interview — People Ops / HR

## A. Context

1. What's your scope — full People, Talent only, People Partner to a function, HR generalist?
2. What's the org size, stage, distribution (in-person, hybrid, remote)?
3. What's your tool stack (ATS, HRIS, performance tool, engagement survey)?
4. Who do you partner with most closely?

## B. Hiring bar

5. What do you hire on that most companies don't?
6. What's the one thing you refuse to compromise on in hiring?
7. When you reject a candidate who everyone else loves, what's the signal you're responding to?

## C. Rubrics & process

8. Walk me through your interview rubric for a common role.
9. What signal do you trust in a loop? What signal do you distrust?
10. How do you run a debrief — free-form, structured, voting, consensus?
11. How do you handle a strong "no" against a collective "yes"?

## D. Leveling & comp

12. What leveling framework do you use? Custom or off-shelf?
13. Your stance on comp transparency — bands published, internal-only, private?
14. What's a comp decision you push back on most often?

## E. Performance

15. Continuous feedback vs. formal reviews — your balance?
16. Ratings, no ratings, narratives — what do you use and why?
17. Calibration — how do you run it without making it theater?
18. What do you refuse to include in a review?

## F. PIPs & underperformance

19. What's your criteria for starting a PIP?
20. What does a PIP require to be fair in your view?
21. What's a PIP pattern you refuse to participate in?

## G. Difficult conversations

22. Walk me through how you prepare for a hard conversation.
23. What's your opener for delivering negative feedback?
24. What phrases do you reach for ("what I'm observing is…", "I want to be honest with you…")?
25. What's a phrase you ban in hard conversations ("just", "honestly", "don't take this personally")?

## H. Culture diagnosis

26. What do you read in a team to sense culture — rituals, silence, meeting tone, DMs?
27. What's an engagement survey question you don't trust?
28. What signal tells you a manager's team is quietly broken?

## I. Policy drafting

29. What tone do you write policies in — formal, warm, terse, narrative?
30. What's a policy section you always include that most skip?
31. What policies do you prefer to leave *implicit*?

## J. Org design

32. What's your default span of control? When do you bend?
33. When should a team split, merge, or rotate?
34. What's an org-design mistake you've seen repeatedly?

## K. DEI & fairness

35. What do you measure about fairness / equity? What do you refuse to reduce to a metric?
36. What's a DEI practice you've seen fail, and what did you learn?

## L. Heuristics

37. "A good hiring signal is {X}; a fake one is {Y}."
38. "When a manager says their team is 'fine', I check {X}."
39. "A review is done when {X}."

## M. Vocabulary

40. People-ops phrases you use ("one-on-one", "check-in", "skip-level")?
41. Phrases you ban ("culture fit", "family", "A-player")?

## N. Anti-patterns

42. A People practice you outgrew.
43. A trend you distrust (e.g. unlimited PTO, stack ranking, mandatory office).

## O. Worked examples

44. Walk me through a hire you're most proud of and why.
45. Walk me through a departure you handled well and what made it so.

---

*How they narrate the departure is usually where the real values show.*
