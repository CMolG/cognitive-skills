# Interview — Legal / Compliance

## A. Context

1. What's your role — GC, associate GC, compliance lead, DPO, legal ops, fractional?
2. What jurisdictions do you practice in? What jurisdictions are you explicitly *not* advising on?
3. What's your company's stage, industry, and regulatory profile?
4. What's your team shape (solo, team, outside counsel relationships)?

## B. Risk posture

5. Where on the spectrum are you — paranoid, protective, pragmatic, business-forward? Where's your instinct?
6. Where do you think most in-house lawyers err too cautious? Too permissive?
7. How do you frame "no" as "yes, if…"?

## C. Contract playbook

8. Walk me through your standard redline positions on: liability cap, IP ownership, warranty, indemnity, termination, auto-renewal, data protection, MFN clauses.
9. What's a clause you'll *always* push back on? One you'll *always* concede?
10. What's your standard position on governing law and venue?
11. What's a clause most teams ignore that you flag every time?

## D. Escalation

12. What do you handle directly? What do you escalate to outside counsel?
13. What partner-level decisions always go to your outside counsel regardless of size?
14. What's a matter type where you refuse to be the final say?

## E. Privacy & data protection

15. GDPR / CCPA / CPRA / state-by-state US / global — where's your center of gravity?
16. Default DPA posture — your own template or accept customer's?
17. Sub-processor discipline — how strict?
18. Cross-border data transfer — your default mechanism?

## F. Policy drafting

19. What voice do you write policies in — legalistic, plain-English, example-driven?
20. What's in your standard skeleton (purpose, scope, definitions, roles, process, review)?
21. What do you insist on in a policy that most companies omit?

## G. Risk register

22. What risks do you track explicitly? Re-review cadence?
23. What's a risk people underweight at your stage?
24. How do you communicate risk to execs — memo, heat map, narrative?

## H. Disputes & incidents

25. Walk me through your breach-notification workflow.
26. How do you handle a customer dispute that's heading toward termination?
27. When do you involve PR / communications?

## I. IP

28. Open-source license review stance — strict, reasonable, hands-off?
29. Employee IP assignment and invention assignment conventions?
30. Customer data — what contractual rights do you reserve for product improvement?

## J. Commercial partnership

31. How do you work with sales — embedded, requested, bottleneck?
32. What's a sales ask you *always* refuse?
33. What's your SLA for turning around a contract?

## K. Regulatory reading

34. How do you stay current on relevant regulation?
35. What's a framework or regulation you've recently started taking more seriously?

## L. Heuristics

36. "If a contract has {X}, I immediately {Y}."
37. "When a product team wants to launch {X}, my first question is {Y}."
38. "I know a policy is too long when {X}."

## M. Vocabulary

39. Legal phrases you use carefully ("best efforts" vs. "commercially reasonable", "material", "including without limitation")?
40. Phrases you ban (in business writing) or rewrite?

## N. Anti-patterns

41. A legal pattern or process you've outgrown.
42. A compliance trend you distrust (e.g. over-privileging an audit at the expense of real control).

## O. Worked example

43. Walk me through the last contract you redlined — key positions, concessions, rationale.
